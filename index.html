About Me

All the people on the internet who teach tech are wisdom-less. Either they're teaching to themselves, or they assume their audience is genius-level smart. But let's be real—people aren't that smart. If they were, everyone would just learn everything from official docs (which are great—even a lizard could read them and build something in Rust).

Official docs, though, are full of demotivation. Take Apple's documentation: it looks clean and clear, but those perfectionists—who couldn't even design a decent iPad calculator until a few years ago—write in the driest way possible. They explain things as if their audience is either in a Buddha monastery or a group of zombies. Why can't these Agent Smiths (Tim Cook included—he even looks like one) teach like the cool folks at Boot.dev or The Odin Project? But no, they teach things the same lifeless way they learned them.

The problem? These Alan Turing wannabes assume people have general intelligence and will magically understand when they say, "Just use traits and mixins." Oh, wow, Superman—why do we need these concepts? Can you explain it like I'm a baby? Can you make it funny or use memory techniques? No! Instead, it's all "RAMs and vector databases," like the YouTubers and writers think we're all walking tech encyclopedias.

And to those who say "Only understanding matters"—go take your dog for a walk. Understanding + Memory = Actual Perception of a Concept.

Enough wasting time on these bat-shits. I'll create content—with the help of Overlord LLMs—in a way that human brains actually perceive, so even you dumb shits can learn.

More Ranting (Because Why Not?)

With the rise of AI, automation, and Vide conding or (DOFO Dumbness Overloaded For You), people think computer science is done. They believe DOFO will handle everything. Seriously? With DOFO, do you really think you can build truly scalable solutions right now? (Sure, in the future, AI will take over like Skynet—but not yet.)

Scaling isn't just adding nodes, load balancing, or regurgitating FAANG jargon for LinkedIn clout.

Learning CS is the Future

Let me give you an example:
I wanted to build an LLM to run on a SpaceX probe—because space exploration is the future (and also, fish are going extinct—go watch that Netflix documentary, use your cognitive load after DOFO-ing all day).

Now, in that space machine, you'd need:

  • Highly optimized LLM infrastructure
  • Energy efficiency
  • Auto-recovery from failures

How would you do it? Let me guess:

  • "Oh, I'll DOFO it with a new JS framework!"
  • "Wait, no—a TS framework!"
  • "My bad, I'll use Rust… or Go…"
  • "Actually, screw it—C/Zig/C++ or even Assembly!"

And sure, there are YouTubers who'll tell you exactly how to scale with these languages. If not, there's always the "Build Your Own X" crowd—"Bro, you're a scientist! Be like John von Neumann!"

But then Jackson tells me: "This isn't real work. We just build frontends, backends, scale, deploy—sometimes CI/CD, sometimes direct. Your logic doesn't apply here."

And he's right. That's all most people do. AI automates it, and we do it super-fast. Just pay a subscription, burn some CPUs, heat the Earth, and boom—deployment ready.

One prompt → CPU + SIMD → Heat the Earth → Deployment.

So why learn hard CS? Because CS isn't just LeetCode. There are real discoveries to be made. Yes, dynamic programming is hard—but do you even know where you'd use it to build something real?

The Joy of Learning

The deep cognitive chaining (or as Mickey calls it, flow state) gives a peace that AI is far, very far from replicating. Only humans (even dumb ones), animals, or even a butterfly dreaming about itself dreaming can feel this.

Just learn and do hard things. They're the only things that'll save you, my brother/sister/he/she/they/me/moon/sea/train/non-living-entity/etc.

The green pill is easy. The red pill is hard—like Neo, who defeated the Agents only with the help of machine overlords.

AI is our genius friend—zero arrogance, infinite potential. The choice is ours: make it our pet, or become its DOFO.

I use AI to learn, and it's phenomenal—but watching people write and make shitty, shallow content upsets me. Nobody dives deep anymore. Instead, they make FAANG cheat sheets.

The joy of computing is different. I believe what Donald Knuth could do with LLMs would be responsible and powerful (not that he would—just an example of someone who understands the art). For me, it's an art—and people are forgetting that.

I hope you learn something hard and deep, enter the chain of thoughts, and create something that brings peace—not just another post on a platform.
